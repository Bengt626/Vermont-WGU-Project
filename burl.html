The Place to Play

Bordering on what was to be (and was for 18 whole days) the 6th great lake in America, Lake Champlain, Burlington is home to a plethora of fun activities, shops, markets, and local events to keep anyone entertained. Having been incorporated on November 22nd, 1864, Burlington has had it's time to grow and prosper into the loverly urban center it is today.

Burlington is also home to 44,595 people, the world's first lake-faring steamboat, and our beloved Ben & Jerry's ice cream. The average household income in Burlington is $64,000 with the individual income being $37,000.

Burlington Awaits

Waiting for you is Lake Champlain. Lake Champlain makes up roughly 4352 miles of northeastern United States and has 9 cities that make up it's borders. Museums, camping, fishing, boating, and exploration are all expected parts of your journey when you visit here.

In the summer, Burlington turns into a sunny town with the Burlington and Chruch Street Marketplaces showing off local vendors. Owing it's weather to it's region of the northeast United States, In the winter, like the entire state of Vermont, Burlington turns into a winter wonderland also offering skiing, snowboarding, and other winter activies.

All in all, with everything Burlington has to offer, this needs to be a place you stop when visiting Vermont, if for nothing more than biking the 3 mile long, incredibly unique Islan Line Trail, which follows an old rail line, the Island Line, through a narrow slit of land crossing Lake Champlain (yet another reason to visit the almost Great Lake).
