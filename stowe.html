Stowe



Beer, snow, and adventure are
the hallmarks that make Stowe
the place to visit

Nature's Wonderland

Since being chartered in 1763 by Benning Wentworth, Stowe has been a destination for outdoor enthusiasts and lovers of winter. The town is known for having friendly people, great breweries brewing local beers and some of th best slopes to go down.

Being in the northern region of Vermont, this rural town relies primarily on the tourism that the area's nature attracts. Stowe has a population of 5,225 people and is approximately 80 miles south of the Canadian border meaning it gets a good amount of traffic from our northern counterparts as well.

What We're Known For

Stowe is full of amazing things to keep you occupied. Want a unique view of the countryside? Take a helicopter tour of the region. Want to relax? Stay at the Stoweflake Mountain Resort and Spa.

Get a thrill of adrenaline with zipline tours and horseback riding or relax with the family playing some minigolf. End the day at any one of the local breweries and you've got yourself a trip you'll never forget!

Fun Facts About Stowe

  1. The average income of Stowe's households is $83,167
  2. Stowe is Vermont's largest town at over 50,000 acres
  3. Stowe also contains Vermont's highest mountain, Mount Mansfield
  4. For the adventurous, you'll find rock and even Ice climbing in Stowe
