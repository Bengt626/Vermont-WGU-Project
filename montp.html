Montpelier

Despite only having just under 10,000
residents and being the country's least
populated capital, Montpelier has a rich
history and vibrant community
to share with all

Seat of Governance

Having been around since August 14th, 1781, you would expect the capital of Vermont to have a much larger population than what it currently boasts. In fact, it's the least populated state capital in America with only just over 8,000 residents. Not only this, but Montpelier has also been proven to be prone to flooding throughout history.

This might lead some to question why Montpelier was chosen? The Central location for not only Vermont, but the northeast portion of the U.S., played the primary factor in the state's municipality's decision for capital.

Outside of being a primarily governing city, Montpelier is also known for insurance, interestingly enough. The town has been home to insurance since 1828 and has a strong presence in the industry today.
