Seat of Governance

Having been incorporated in August 14th, 1781, you would expect the capital of Vermont to have a much larger population than what it currently boasts. In fact, it's the least populated state capital in America with only 8,023 residents as of 2022. Not only this, but Montpelier has also been proven to be prone to flooding throughout history.

This might lead some to question why Montpelier was chosen? The Central location for not only Vermont, but the northeast portion of the U.S., played the primary factor in the state's municipality's decision for capital.

Outside of being a primarily governing city, Montpelier is also known for insurance, interestingly enough. The town has been home to insurance since 1828 and has a strong presence in the industry today.

Montpelier Today

Montpelier has come to be a place its residents can enjoy living. With and average household income of about $76,000, the residents can live comfortably in the suburban, bordering rural town, with people have the space to spread out.

While our capital may not be big an bustling like others, there are still plenty of reasons to come visit Montpelier!

Know Your Montpelier History!

Here are a few facts from Montpelier's past

  1. The first home was built in 1787
  2. By 1791, the population had reached 113 people
  3. In the early days, Native Americans played a large part in the towns commerce
  4. In 1895, local census shows over 45% immigrant population bolstering growth
  5. National LIfe Insurance Company was the catalyst for a lot of early growth when HQ moved here in 1848
